# Folio Swiss Noir

Use when the deck needs a harder exhibition-folio voice: black, paper, strong
rules, and proof surfaces that read from the back of the room.

- Build with black/white structure and one restrained amber cue.
- Use poster covers, ledger evidence, hard borders, and exact alignment.
- Keep type large and sans; hierarchy should survive a squint test.
- Screenshots are exhibits, not thumbnails. If an artifact is not readable,
  remove it or give it a full proof slot.
- The deck should feel edited and printed, not generated.

Anti-patterns:

- soft shadow stacks, pastel dashboard cards, neon HUD effects
- dense body copy in poster pages
- decorative icons that do not encode evidence or categories
