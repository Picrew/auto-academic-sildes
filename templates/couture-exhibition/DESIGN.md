# Couture Exhibition

Use when a deck needs a high-end web/editorial exhibition feeling: fewer
objects, larger proof surfaces, strong typography, and image plates that feel
curated instead of templated.

## Grammar

- Ivory page, charcoal ink, muted gallery accent.
- Didot/Bodoni/Georgia display titles with quiet sans body text.
- One proof surface should feel like an exhibit plate, not a screenshot pasted
  into a card.
- Irregular bento/index slides are allowed only when each cell has evidence.
- No neon, no glassmorphism, no decorative 3D, no stock-like atmosphere.

## Layout

- Cover: `cover-title-wall`
- Evidence: `proof-gallery-split`
- Artifact content: `artifact-showcase`
- Labels: `content-bento`
- Matrix: `matrix-map`

## Image Rules

- Prefer one strong crop over many small thumbnails.
- Captions must read like exhibition wall labels: what to inspect and where it
  came from.
- Wide screenshots need tight crops; letterboxed plates should fail review.
