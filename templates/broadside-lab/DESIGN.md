# Broadside Lab

Use for strong academic/technical stories that need editorial force: tool-use
systems, benchmarks, post-training, infrastructure, or public profiles with a
clear research stance.

## Sources

- Huashu Broadside and critique guide: dramatic but functional typography,
  single accent, five-dimensional review, no cliché AI effects.
- Experimental Jetset: typography and grid become the image.
- JS.Design high-sense page pattern: high-end feeling comes from confident
  composition and selected references, not decoration.

## Visual Grammar

- Near-black editorial canvas, ivory type, one fire-orange accent.
- Large sans-serif action titles; rules and tables are visible.
- Source screenshots sit in strict proof windows, never floating glass cards.
- Density target: 50-68% useful fill; dark negative space must frame proof, not
  hide missing content.

## Slide Jobs

- Cover: manifesto-like claim, compact artifact, metric anchors.
- Matrix: strong ledger with visible row rhythm.
- Evidence: proof window dominates with compact side notes.
- Close: compact thesis, no thank-you slide.

## Avoid

- Neon sci-fi, particles, gradients, random glow, fake dashboards, emoji.
- Using the dark stage for weak claims.
- More than one strong accent per page.
