# Object Study Wall

Use when a project, paper, or researcher has a concrete public artifact that can
anchor the deck visually.

## Reference DNA

- High-end product/editorial pages: one object, strong crop, disciplined copy.
- PRISM identity rail for academic credibility.
- Gallery wall rhythm rather than landing-page hero.

## Grammar

- Warm gallery surface, charcoal text, cobalt accent, occasional dark wall.
- Covers and proof pages privilege the object/source surface.
- Content pages may use bento blocks, but only as labeled evidence groups.
- Screenshots should be cropped like objects under study, not browser dumps.
- Avoid stock-like backgrounds, 3D ornaments, and decorative thumbnails.

## Best Fit

- Project profiles.
- Research portfolios with demos, repos, leaderboards, or homepages.
- Short external-facing technical briefings.
