# Prism Workbench Index

Use this grammar when a researcher, lab, or project deck has enough selected
works to behave like a public academic homepage rather than a resume.

## Visual Grammar

- Near-white page, graphite text, muted sage hairlines, and a restrained brass
  accent for source/index numbers.
- Serif action titles with compact sans supporting copy.
- Cover uses a left identity/source rail and a right claim/credibility block.
- Content pages use `content-workbench-index`: a compact claim column plus
  selected-work rows, avoiding card-grid repetition.
- Proof pages keep a dossier rhythm: source context on the left, readable
  homepage/project/paper crop on the right.

## Use

- Public researcher profiles with projects, papers, datasets, or benchmark
  surfaces.
- Academic homepage to deck transformations.
- Source-linked portfolio reviews where the deck should feel precise, not loud.

## Avoid

- Marketing hero copy.
- Rounded card stacks that make the profile look like a template.
- Cover screenshots as proof. Bare cover `image` should stay a compact identity
  anchor; use `evidence:` for any cover screenshot that proves a claim.
