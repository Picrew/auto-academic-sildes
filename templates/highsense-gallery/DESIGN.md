# Highsense Gallery

For profile, product-research, and public-source decks that need the polish of
high-end web editorial without becoming a landing page.

## Feel

Airy, curated, and image-literate. The deck should feel like a small exhibition:
each slide has one surface worth looking at and one judgment worth remembering.

## Layout

- cover: title as the main object, one small source image or no image
- signal slide: large typography plus 2-3 metrics, not a feature list
- evidence slide: one oversized proof window, cropped to the meaningful region
- comparison slide: gallery strip or two strong panes, never many equal cards
- close: compact thesis with a quiet source trail

## Visual Choices

- luminous background wash with neutral text blocks
- accent gradients only as rules, small bands, or proof halos
- sans headline with generous line-height and tight alignment
- screenshots framed as evidence windows, not decorative browser chrome
- section cadence varies by scale: poster title, proof wall, compact map

## Avoid

- copying a reference page's brand style
- glassy nested cards, random glows, and floating widgets
- portraits as hero imagery unless identity is the evidence
- overusing pastel gradients; one or two contrast spikes per deck is enough
