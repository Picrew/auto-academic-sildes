# Signal Intelligence Brief

Source influence: Beautiful HTML Templates `signal`, PRISM source architecture,
and institutional editorial briefings.

Use when a research profile, public-source audit, or technical briefing needs
quiet authority rather than a visual reset.

## Visual Grammar

- Warm cream paper and editorial navy, with antique gold used only as a signal.
- Serif headings carry judgment; sans body stays compact; mono labels mark
  provenance, timestamps, methods, and source rows.
- Slides should feel like briefing pages: one claim, one evidence exhibit, and
  a compact source ledger.
- Density is welcome when each row has a comparison or provenance job.
- Avoid generic investor-deck polish, blue-purple AI gradients, and decorative
  icons.

## Layout Defaults

- Cover: `cover-source-rail`
- Proof: `proof-atlas-spread`
- Artifact: `artifact-marginalia`
- Matrix: `matrix-ledger`
- Content with labels: `content-marginalia`

## Evidence Rule

Every screenshot, table, repo, paper page, or project page should be readable as
a briefing exhibit. If it cannot support source-level inspection, demote it to a
small identity cue or remove it.
