# JS Editorial Cascade

Use for public research profiles, project decks, and technical stories that
need high-end web energy without becoming a landing page.

## Grammar

- Asymmetric editorial cascade: one large content/proof protagonist plus a
  compact evidence/index rail.
- Ivory surface, charcoal ink, one electric blue source marker, and one warm
  orange decision accent.
- Heavy sans headlines, tight source labels, and large proof plates replace
  generic cards.
- Solid color bands and thick rules create rhythm; no gradients, blobs, glass,
  fake dashboards, or stock abstraction.

## Layout

- Cover: `cover-title-wall`
- Evidence: `proof-stage`
- Artifact content: `artifact-showcase`
- Matrix: `matrix-map`
- Labels: `content-bento`

## Image Rules

- Use one source surface as the protagonist; crop until the claim is readable.
- Artifact slides should feel like a web feature story: small claim rail, large
  screenshot, caption/source as the receipt.
- Keep callouts sparse and aligned to the source image.
- If the page feels empty, enlarge the proof plate or convert labels into a
  bento/index module rather than adding decoration.
