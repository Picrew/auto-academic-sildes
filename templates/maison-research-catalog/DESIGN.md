# Maison Research Catalog

Use when a researcher or project profile needs high-end editorial catalog energy
without becoming a consumer landing page.

- Treat screenshots, project pages, and papers as catalog plates with quiet wall
  labels.
- Use warm paper, deep ink, and one oxblood source cue; avoid gradients and
  luxury-stock atmosphere.
- Let one source artifact dominate proof and artifact slides. Side text should
  be a compact index, not a second essay.
- Prefer asymmetry: a small claim rail against a large source plate.
- Keep cards flat and rectangular; borders and typography provide craft.

Anti-patterns:

- generic AI gradients, glass cards, floating blobs, decorative portraits
- half-empty title pages with tiny source thumbnails
- multiple unrelated screenshots on one slide
