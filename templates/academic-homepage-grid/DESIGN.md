# Academic Homepage Grid

For researcher profiles and public-source project decks that should feel like a
polished academic homepage rather than a poster or AI slide template.

## Inspiration

PRISM-style academic pages: a stable identity rail, serif/sans typography,
publication cards, compact news rows, and enough information density to feel
useful at a glance.

## Layout

- cover: left identity/source rail, right research thesis and credibility anchors
- stack: left claim, right interest/project tiles so the slide does not feel empty
- matrix: compact publication/project table with an index rail
- evidence: homepage or artifact screenshot as a large readable card
- close: synthesis plus links, not a giant empty thank-you slide

## Visual Choices

- near-white background, deep slate text, warm gold accent
- serif headings, Inter-like sans body, small mono metadata
- information cards with thin borders and 8px radius
- large enough type to read from a contact sheet
- profile images and logos are anchors, not hero decoration

## Avoid

- giant blank canvases with a few tiny bullets
- copying PRISM branding or sample content
- glass, glow, random gradients, and fake dashboards
- using homepage screenshots when a publication/result artifact proves the claim better
