# Atlas Marginalia

Use when the deck should feel like an annotated academic atlas: source-first,
quiet, precise, and readable.

## Reference DNA

- Information Architects: content before surface.
- Paper atlases: plate, caption, marginal note, citation line.
- PRISM: source-linked academic identity without marketing treatment.

## Grammar

- Near-white page, black text, classic blue source accent.
- Proof pages place the figure/page as the primary plate with a narrow marginal
  rail.
- Captions and pins should read like editorial annotations, not UI labels.
- Content pages use side marginalia instead of floating cards.
- Keep line length and citation surfaces calm; no decorative illustration.

## Best Fit

- Paper talks, literature reviews, public profiles with papers.
- Scholar/project decks where provenance matters more than stage drama.
