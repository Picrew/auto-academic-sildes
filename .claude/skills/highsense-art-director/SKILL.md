# Highsense Art Director Skill

Use when a deck needs a sharper visual point of view before or during HTML-first slide design.

## Workflow

1. Gather 3-6 strong references from high-end websites, editorial pages, product launches, research labs, portfolios, or conference talks. Prefer real designed artifacts over slide templates.
2. For each reference, extract only transferable grammar: grid, density, type scale, whitespace, image treatment, color rhythm, caption style, and motion/section cadence. Ignore brand copy and decorative tricks.
3. Distill a short visual brief before building slides:
   - Deck mood and audience.
   - Layout system and spacing rules.
   - Evidence image treatment and crop strategy.
   - Palette, accent cadence, and typography hierarchy.
   - Reusable slide types: cover, section break, evidence close-up, comparison, method, result.
4. Apply the grammar in HTML/CSS, then render and inspect the contact sheet.
5. Iterate one high-leverage variable per pass: grid, crop, scale, density, color, or typography.

## Judgment Rules

- Borrow structure, pacing, and restraint; do not copy a reference's brand surface verbatim.
- Academic and technical slides must foreground claims and proof, not marketing hero composition.
- Evidence images should be large, tightly cropped, and legible enough to support the slide claim.
- Whitespace should create hierarchy. Empty areas that do not improve reading should be rebalanced.
- Use color rhythm: mostly neutral structure, limited accents, and intentional contrast spikes for evidence or section turns.
- Avoid generic AI styling: purple-blue gradients, glassy cards, random blobs, ornamental glow, fake dashboards, stock abstraction, and decorative 3D unless they directly explain the content.
- Do not fill slides with nested cards. Use bands, columns, crop windows, callouts, and typographic contrast.
- Keep annotations sparse and aligned. A pin, bracket, or label must make a judgment clearer.
- If the deck starts looking like a template, change the layout cadence before changing colors.

## Output

When reporting direction or review notes, use:

- `Visual brief`: the selected grammar in 3-6 concrete bullets.
- `Keep`: choices that should survive the next render.
- `Change`: specific slide-level or CSS-level edits.
- `Risk`: anything likely to read as generic, decorative, or illegible.
